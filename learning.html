The First Rule of Prompt Club

The first rule of Prompt Club is: you talk to the AI like a helpful, super-smart friend. A prompt is just a clear and friendly instruction. The better the instruction, the better the result.

We'll start with plain English, add some helpful context, give it a few clues about the format we want, and add any rules it needs to follow. By the end of this, you'll know exactly how to go from a fuzzy idea to a brilliant prompt.

Anatomy of a Great Prompt

1. The Task

Start with a clear instruction. Tell the AI what you want it to do: `explain`, `create`, `summarise`, `translate`.

2. The Persona

Ask the AI to act out a role. This helps it adopt the right tone of voice and level of knowledge for your task.

3. The Context

Give the AI the background information it needs. The more detail you provide, the less the AI has to guess.

4. The Format

Describe how you want the output to look: a numbered list, a table, a poem, a script, and so on.

Sample Prompt

Act as a museum curator preparing a guided tour for teenagers who love science fiction. Summarise three important artefacts from British space exploration in a fun timeline, include one quirky fact per artefact, and finish with a thoughtful question for the group.

Use this simple framework to structure your prompts effectively:

C - Context

Provide background information and set the scene

R - Role

Define who the AI should act as (expert, teacher, friend)

A - Action

Clearly state what you want the AI to do

F - Format

Specify how you want the output structured

T - Tone

Set the voice and style of the response

Example using CRAFT:

"You are an experienced fitness coach (Role). I'm a 35-year-old beginner who wants to lose weight (Context). Create a 4-week workout plan (Action) as a weekly schedule with exercises, sets, and reps (Format). Use encouraging and motivational language (Tone)."
