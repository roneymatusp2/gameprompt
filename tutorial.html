The First Rule of Prompt Club

The first rule of Prompt Club is: you talk to the AI like a helpful, super-smart friend. A prompt is just a clear and friendly instruction. The better the instruction, the better the result.

We'll start with plain English, add some helpful context, give it a few clues about the format we want, and add any rules it needs to follow. By the end of this, you'll know exactly how to go from a fuzzy idea to a brilliant prompt.

Anatomy of a Great Prompt

1. The Task

Start with a clear instruction. Tell the AI what you want it to do: `explain`, `create`, `summarise`, `translate`.

2. The Persona

Ask the AI to act out a role. This helps it adopt the right tone of voice and level of knowledge for your task.

3. The Context

Give the AI the background information it needs. The more detail you provide, the less the AI has to guess.

4. The Format

Describe how you want the output to look: a numbered list, a table, a poem, a script, and so on.

Sample Prompt

Act as a museum curator preparing a guided tour for teenagers who love science fiction. Summarise three important artefacts from British space exploration in a fun timeline, include one quirky fact per artefact, and finish with a thoughtful question for the group.

The Art of Polishing Your Prompts

  1. 1. Draft: Get your first idea down quickly. Don't worry about making it perfect first time.
  2. 2. Inspect: Check the AI's response. What worked? What went a bit weird?
  3. 3. Refine: Add more detail, tighten your rules, or clarify the format you want.
  4. 4. Challenge: Ask the AI to critique its own answer or offer different ideas.
  5. 5. Record: Save your best prompts in a notebook or file so you can use them again later.

Your Prompting Toolkit

Good Habits

  • ‚Ä¢Use confident verbs like `create`, `summarise`, `critique`, or `compare`.
  • ‚Ä¢Mention the audience: "for a primary school assembly", "in a friendly, reassuring voice".
  • ‚Ä¢Paste in the data you want the AI to analyse instead of just talking about it.
  • ‚Ä¢Break big tasks into numbered steps so the AI doesn't get lost.
  • ‚Ä¢Ask the AI to explain its reasoning so you can check its work.

Traps to Avoid

  • ‚Ä¢Don't assume the AI remembers your last chat. Always start fresh.
  • ‚Ä¢Avoid asking for five different things in one massive prompt.
  • ‚Ä¢Steer clear of vague words like "nice" or "better" without explaining what you mean.
  • ‚Ä¢Don't say what you *don't* want ("don't be boring"); say what you *do* want ("be exciting").
  • ‚Ä¢Don't stop after one good response. Keep refining to see if you can make it even better!

Common Mistakes (and How to Fix Them)

1. The Mind-Reading Mistake

Expecting the AI to remember what you talked about yesterday. It can't! Always remind it of the context.

‚ùå "Carry on with the idea we had before."

Instead: Summarise the idea in one sentence before asking the next thing.

2. The Everything-But-the-Kitchen-Sink Prompt

Stuffing a single prompt with loads of unrelated requests.

‚ùå "Write my brochure, design the cover, and plan the social media launch."

Instead: Break the job into smaller, separate prompts. One thing at a time!

3. The "Guess What I'm Thinking" Prompt

Forgetting to give the AI important details like the length, tone, or format you want.

‚ùå "Draft a letter to parents."

Instead: Add the tone ("warm but authoritative"), the length ("no more than 200 words"), and the key message.

A Quick Word on AI Ethics

Being a great prompt engineer isn't just about getting cool results. It's also about being a responsible user of AI. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • ‚Ä¢Fairness: AI models learn from the internet, which can sometimes be biased. Always question the AI's answers and think about whether they might be unfair to certain groups of people.
  • ‚Ä¢Privacy: Don't share personal or sensitive information with an AI. Treat it like a public forum.
  • ‚Ä¢Honesty: Don't use AI to cheat on your homework or to create fake information. Use it as a tool to help you learn and create, not to deceive others.
